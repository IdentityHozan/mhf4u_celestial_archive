Parent Function\[ y = \frac{1}{x^2} \]

Transformations Applied

To accurately track the swift curve of the flying cape drawing on the bus mural, transformations were applied to the parent function \( y = \frac{1}{x^2} \) in strict mathematical order:

  • Horizontal Translation (\( d = \sqrt{142} \approx 11.92 \)): First, the function was translated approximately 11.92 units to the right, changing the denominator structure to \( x^2 - 142 \). This shifts the parent function's natural vertical asymptote away from the origin to \( x = \sqrt{142} \). This positioning aligns the sharp drop of the right branch with the steep, outer edge of the character's flying cape.
  • Vertical Translation (\( c = 3.3 \)): Next, the entire graph was shifted 3.3 units upward. This vertical translation elevates the curve's baseline to align with the physical height of the cape graphic relative to the lower edge of the bus chassis.

Physical Constraints and Asymptotic Behaviour

The parent function \( y = \frac{1}{x^2} \) has an infinite domain where \( x \neq 0 \), a range of \( y > 0 \), and a vertical asymptote running along the y-axis. In this transformed real-world model, the function contains two mathematical discontinuities where the denominator equals zero, producing vertical asymptotes at \( x = \pm\sqrt{142} \) (or roughly \( x \pm 11.92 \)). Because our model utilizes only the positive branch, the key operational boundary is the vertical asymptote at \( x \approx 11.92 \).

Vertical Asymptotes\[ x = \pm\sqrt{142} \approx \pm11.92 \]
Horizontal Asymptote\[ y = 3.3 \]

For the webpage presentation, the domain is strictly limited to the specific boundaries where the character's cape is actively drawn across the background panels:

\[ D: \{x \in \mathbb{R} \mid 11.94 \le x \le 14.2\} \]

Since the rational function is completely continuous and decreasing over this particular window, the maximum and minimum elevations are found directly by evaluating our tracking endpoints:

  • Maximum Cape Height: The highest boundary point occurs near the asymptote at \( x = 11.94 \), where \( f(11.94) \approx 5.0743 \).
  • Minimum Cape Height: As the cape curve flattens out towards the right, evaluating the endpoint at \( x = 14.2 \) yields an approximate height of \( f(14.2) \approx 3.3168 \).

This contextual restriction alters the infinite range of the parent function into a restricted real-world interval. This prevents the mathematical model from extending up into empty air space or down past the physical layout coordinates where the illustration terminates.
